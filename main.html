In India, sexual and reproductive health is centered around maternal care. In fact, the only time when most women have ever visited a gynecologist is when they are pregnant. Unmarried women are even more judged and shamed for their sexuality. A survey Health Over Stigma conducted by the Delhi-based Non Profit Organization Haiyya revealed that only 1% of women have received information on sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR) from their mothers, doctors or government campaigns. It showed that unmarried women are often denied services, misinformed or coerced into decisions. 95% of unmarried women had never visited a gynaecologist to take consultation on sex, pleasure or contraception. The stigma around reproductive and sexual health and well being only worsens in rural areas. A study by the Indian Council of Medical Research of 93,356 married women in rural areas showed that only 20.6% of women knew the availability of services for gynaecological problems. Out of the 14.2% women who visited some health facility for their gynaecological problems only 9.8% felt satisfied with the availed services. In India, unsafe abortions account for about 3,500 women dying every year. Abortion complications are the third major cause of maternal death, after haemorrhage and sepsis. And it goes without saying that the case is even worse for members of the LGBTQ community. As studied by the UNAID, there is also a staggering amount of Discrimination, Stigmatization and Denial of health services to HIV/AIDS patients.

This is our attempt to help women from all parts of the country connect with trustworthy, respectful, well trained gynecologists and get access to quality sexual and reproductive healthcare without any judgement.
