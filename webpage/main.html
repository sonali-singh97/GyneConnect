In India, sexual and reproductive health is centered around maternal care. In fact, the only time when most women have ever visited a gynecologist is when they are pregnant. Unmarried women are even more judged and shamed for their sexuality.
A survey Health Over Stigma conducted by the Delhi-based Non Profit Organization Haiyya revealed that only 1% of women have received information on sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR) from their mothers, doctors or government campaigns. It showed that unmarried women are often denied services, misinformed or coerced into decisions. 95% of unmarried women had never visited a gynecologist to take consultation on sex, pleasure or contraception.


The stigma around reproductive and sexual health and well being only worsens in rural areas. A study by the Indian Council of Medical Research of 93,356 married women in rural areas showed that only 20.6% of women knew the availability of services for gynecological problems. Out of the 14.2% women who visited some health facility for their gynecological problems only 9.8% felt satisfied with the availed services.
In India, unsafe abortions account for about 3,500 women dying every year. Abortion complications are the third major cause of maternal death, after haemorrhage and sepsis. And it goes without saying that the case is even worse for members of the LGBTQ community. As studied by the UNAID, there is also a staggering amount of Discrimination, Stigmatization and Denial of health services to HIV/AIDS patients.

This is our attempt to help women from all parts of the country connect with trustworthy, respectful, well-trained gynecologists and get access to quality sexual and reproductive healthcare without any judgement.

Instructions for using Gyne Connect


To find a gynecologist in your city, go to the Find a gynecologist tab, enter your city, and choose the most suitable doctor for you.
To help you make the best decision, we have provided a variety of tags, information and reviews of each of the doctors.
To submit a review, go to the Review tab. We encourage you to provide feedback for each of the appointments you have made, so the reviews remain as accurate as possible. We sincerely request you to only fill this form if you have actually consulted with the doctor.
For an online consultation, you can book an appointment with one of the doctors who have registered on the website for a small fee (or for free for some doctors!) in one of the available time slots. However, it is strongly recommend you to use this service only in case of emergencies, as the physical examination would provide more accurate results.
If you find any discrepancies for any doctor listed here, fill the Report form so that it can be rectified.

For Gynecologists:
If you consider yourself to be one of the trustworthy, respectful, non-judgemental doctors here, please register on the Sign up as a doctor page.
You will be asked to enter some details, after which you will be thoroughly verified. Once verified, your account will be created and you will receive an email, after which you can consult patients for a fee of your choosing at your preferred timings.
